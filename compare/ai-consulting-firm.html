The strategy-to-production gap

The problem is what comes next: your engineering team now owns implementation of a system in a domain most of them have never shipped before. The 67% failure-to-production rate is not a planning failure. It is a delivery failure.

An AI services company takes a different accountability position. FAQ

What is the difference between an AI consulting firm and an AI services company?

An AI consulting firm delivers strategy: roadmaps, assessments, architecture recommendations, and implementation plans. An AI services company like Altor delivers production: a working AI system deployed in your stack, accountable for measurable outcomes. Consulting ends with a document; services ends with a system running on your data.

Why do 67% of enterprise AI projects fail to reach production?

The primary reason is the gap between strategy and implementation. Most AI consulting engagements produce a roadmap and then hand off implementation to a team that lacks the AI expertise to execute it. The plan is sound; the production system never ships. AI services companies close this gap by being accountable for production, not just the plan.

How long does a typical AI consulting engagement take?

An AI consulting engagement for a workflow automation initiative typically runs 3-6 months and ends with a strategy document and architecture plan. A production AI system deployment with Altor takes 14 days from kickoff.

When does consulting make more sense than services?

Consulting makes sense when you need an AI strategy across multiple business units, board-level buy-in on an AI roadmap, or organizational readiness assessment before committing to builds. Services make sense when you have identified a specific workflow and need it automated in production.
