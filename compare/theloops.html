Where Altor Wins

TheLoops helps teams sort and understand support work faster, which can improve operations meaningfully. But triage quality and technical diagnosis are different jobs. A ticket can be classified perfectly and still require a human to open five tools to determine what actually broke.

Altor is aimed at that second problem. It automates the evidence gathering and hypothesis generation needed for technical issues, reducing the dependence on ad hoc support engineering investigation.

Where TheLoops Wins

TheLoops wins if your main goal is smarter triage, knowledge workflows, and support intelligence at the queue level. That is a valuable capability for mature support ops teams.

It may also be the better fit if your issues are not deeply technical but you still want AI to improve ticket understanding and process efficiency.

Who Should Choose What

Choose Altor if: your slowest tickets are slow because diagnosis requires backend evidence and support-engineering effort.

Choose TheLoops if: you want AI-driven triage, support intelligence, and queue-level efficiency improvements.

FAQ

What's the difference between Altor and TheLoops?

TheLoops focuses on support intelligence and triage. Altor focuses on automated technical investigation across live systems.

Is Altor better than TheLoops for B2B support?

Altor is better for root-cause diagnosis. TheLoops may be better for queue intelligence and triage optimization.

How much does TheLoops cost vs Altor?

TheLoops is typically quote-based, while Altor uses usage-based investigation pricing.

Can Altor and TheLoops be used together?

Yes. TheLoops can improve triage quality and summaries while Altor handles the product and infrastructure investigation behind harder technical cases.

Which product is better for queue-level efficiency?

TheLoops is better for queue-level efficiency and agent guidance. Altor is better for the actual root-cause work once a case has been identified as technical.

When TheLoops is the right choice

TheLoops is the better choice when the support organization wants to improve queue performance before it changes the deeper investigation workflow. Its value is strongest when teams need better classification, summaries, routing, and agent guidance so frontline work becomes more consistent and easier to manage.

  • You need smarter triage across a large queue. If the biggest issue is that tickets are poorly labeled, routed late, or inconsistent in quality, TheLoops is closer to the root problem.
  • Your tickets are varied, but not always deeply technical. Many teams benefit from better AI guidance and queue understanding even when only a smaller share of cases require real engineering-style diagnosis.
  • You want agent efficiency gains without reworking your service stack. TheLoops fits teams looking to improve how support operates at the queue level first.

When Altor is the right choice

Altor is the better choice when triage is not the main bottleneck. In many B2B products, the team already knows which tickets are serious. What takes time is proving what happened in production and writing an answer that is grounded in logs, state changes, bug history, and account-specific facts.

  • Your slowest tickets are slow because of investigation, not classification. If the same technically hard issues keep forcing agents into manual digging, the queue does not need better labeling as much as it needs faster diagnosis.
  • You need direct access to the systems where the issue happened. Altor is built for live production reads across multiple sources, which is a different depth from queue intelligence.
  • You want the investigation path to improve from repeated cases. Over time, playbooks can narrow in on likely root causes faster because they are grounded in what the data showed on prior tickets.

If the question is less about queue hygiene and more about finding cause quickly, review support investigation for a closer look at the workflow Altor supports.
