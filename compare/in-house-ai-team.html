The real build vs buy calculation

Most B2B SaaS companies ask this question when a specific workflow is costing $80-200K per year in manual engineering time. The instinct is often "let's hire someone." But hiring a senior AI engineer in the US in 2026 means $180-250K in base salary, $30-60K in benefits and overhead, a 3-6 month recruiting cycle, and then another 3-6 months before a production system is live. Total cost to first result: $250-400K+ and up to a year.

An AI services company deploys a working production system in 14 days. Usage-based pricing means you pay for actual workflow throughput, not a salary. For a single high-cost workflow, the math usually favors the services model unless AI is core to your product roadmap and you expect to automate 5+ workflows in year one.

When to hire vs when to use services

Hire your own AI team when…

AI is central to your product roadmap. You need 5+ workflows automated. You have an existing engineering org that can support the infrastructure. You are building a data moat that compounds over years. Budget is not a constraint and timeline is flexible.

Use an AI services company when…

You have one specific high-cost workflow — support investigation, ticket triage, billing diagnosis — costing $80K+ per year in engineer time. You need it automated in production this quarter, not next year. You want accountability, not a platform to operate.

FAQ

Should I hire an AI engineer or use an AI services company?

It depends on your timeline and scope. Hiring an AI engineer costs $180-250K/year in total compensation, takes 3-6 months to close the role, and then 2-4 months to design, build, and deploy a system. An AI services company like Altor deploys a production system in 14 days at usage-based pricing. For a specific workflow you need automated fast, the services model typically delivers faster ROI.

What is the real cost of hiring an AI engineer in 2026?

Total cost of a senior AI engineer in the US in 2026: $180-250K base, plus $30-60K benefits and overhead, plus 3-6 months recruiting time and opportunity cost. First production system typically takes 3-6 months after hire. Total cost to first production AI system: $250-400K+ and 6-12 months.

When does hiring make more sense than an AI services company?

Hiring your own AI team makes sense when you have 5+ workflows to automate, AI is core to your product roadmap, and you have an existing engineering team large enough to support the infrastructure work. For a single high-impact workflow, a services model delivers faster and cheaper.
