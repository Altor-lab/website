Altor vs Gorgias

Altor is built for B2B AI support ticket investigation: it pulls live technical context, checks known bugs, and returns a probable diagnosis instead of just drafting a reply.

TL;DR: Gorgias is better for ecommerce support tied to orders, refunds, and storefront workflows. Altor is better for B2B SaaS teams where tickets depend on API behavior, account configuration, or technical backend context.

Where Altor Wins

Gorgias is excellent at turning order and storefront data into support context. That is exactly what ecommerce teams need. B2B SaaS support usually has a different shape: webhook failures, auth issues, usage anomalies, feature-flag confusion, and product regressions tied to specific tenants.

Altor is built for that B2B pattern. It can connect to engineering and product systems, investigate the issue automatically, and give support a root-cause-oriented answer rather than just account metadata.

Where Gorgias Wins

Gorgias wins if ecommerce is your core workflow. Its native store context, macros, and automation are far more relevant than a generalized technical investigation layer.

It is also better for high-volume consumer-style support, where speed on order-related questions matters more than support engineering depth.

Who Should Choose What

Choose Altor if: you support a technical B2B product and need evidence from logs, billing, bugs, or code systems.

Choose Gorgias if: you run ecommerce support and want automation tied directly to stores, orders, and refunds.

FAQ

What's the difference between Altor and Gorgias?

Gorgias is built around ecommerce support operations. Altor is built around technical investigation for B2B software support.

Is Altor better than Gorgias for B2B support?

For B2B support investigation, yes. For ecommerce support, Gorgias is usually better.

How much does Gorgias cost vs Altor?

Gorgias often prices by ticket volume or plan tier; Altor prices by investigation usage.
