What this comparison is really about

Freshdesk AI is useful when teams want a general-purpose service desk layer with suggested responses, routing, and agent productivity features. Those benefits matter, but they do not automatically solve high-context technical issues. If the core question is why a request failed for one enterprise customer after yesterday's release, the answer is not in workflow automation alone. The answer lives in the systems behind the ticket.

Investigation matters because workflow quality does not equal root-cause quality. A perfectly routed ticket can still sit for hours while someone gathers screenshots, checks Datadog, searches Jira, or asks engineering to validate a theory. Altor removes that waiting period by collecting the first layer of truth automatically. Support starts from evidence, not from a queue state.

FAQ

What is the difference between Altor and Freshdesk AI?

Altor is built to investigate account-specific technical issues across your stack. Freshdesk AI is built to automate service-desk work such as routing, summarization, suggested replies, and self-service.

Which is better for B2B technical support?

Altor is usually better for B2B technical support teams that need evidence from production systems before replying. Freshdesk AI is usually better for general ticket operations where speed inside the help desk matters more than root-cause depth.

How does Freshdesk AI handle ticket investigation?

Freshdesk AI usually works from ticket context, support history, and knowledge assets in the Freshworks environment. It can improve handling speed, but it does not normally inspect live databases, billing systems, and bug trackers at the same time.

Can Altor replace Freshdesk AI?

Altor can replace manual technical investigation, but it is not a full service-desk replacement. Teams that rely on Freshdesk for queue management, SLAs, and omnichannel support may keep Freshdesk and add Altor for complex escalations.

What does Freshdesk AI cost vs Altor?

Freshdesk AI pricing usually follows seat-based plans and feature tiers inside Freshworks. Altor charges per investigation, which can be a better match when the highest cost sits in hard technical tickets rather than seat count.

When to Choose Freshdesk AI

Choose Freshdesk AI if you want a broad service desk with automation built in and your team already lives in the Freshworks ecosystem. It is a sensible option when the main problems are ticket categorization, routing, SLA handling, summarization, or response drafting for repeat issues. Teams with mixed workloads across customer support and internal service management can get real value from that breadth.

Freshdesk AI is also a fair choice for smaller or mid-sized teams that need one system to cover many workflows well enough. If your support motion includes email, chat, call center tasks, and general admin requests, using one platform for operations can be more practical than adding a product focused only on diagnosis.

And if most of your tickets stop at the help desk, Freshdesk AI may be the right place to invest first. A company that rarely touches logs, billing records, or bug trackers during support work will likely see more immediate value from better service-desk operations than from deep investigation tooling.

When to Choose Altor

Choose Altor when the tickets that hurt most are the ones that bounce between support and engineering. That usually means account-specific issues with missing events, failed provisioning, broken entitlements, invoice state drift, or regressions after a deploy. In those cases, better ticket summaries help, but they do not answer the customer's actual question.

Altor pulls that answer from the stack. It queries ClickHouse, Linear, Stripe, GitHub, and related systems in one pass so support can see whether the problem ties back to data arrival, billing state, a known bug, or a recent change in code. The product was built by an ex-Microsoft AI team for investigation-heavy support, so the workflow starts with evidence collection rather than with inbox handling.

That is why Altor fits B2B engineering teams with 200 or more tickets a month. You keep Freshdesk if you like it for the queue, but Altor handles the part people usually do by hand: opening five tools, building a theory, checking whether it is true, and then writing a customer-safe explanation. That can cut time to resolution much more than another routing rule.

McKinsey found a 14% lift in issues resolved per hour and a 9% drop in time spent per issue in a customer service deployment using generative AI (McKinsey, 2023). That is a strong case for service-desk automation, especially for repetitive work where Freshdesk AI is strongest.

IBM reported a 64% average containment rate for virtual agent programs, plus a 12% drop in human handle time (IBM Institute for Business Value). Those gains are real, but they matter most when the remaining ticket set is also handled well. For technical B2B support, that usually means improving diagnosis after containment reaches its limit.

That is a useful benchmark when you compare general automation to investigation products. The easy contacts move first. The costly contacts stay manual unless you change how investigation happens.
