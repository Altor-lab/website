What this comparison is really about

Both Turing and Altor surface when B2B teams search for ways to build AI systems faster. But they are structurally different. Turing is a talent marketplace: you hire vetted engineers who join your team and work on whatever you direct them to. Altor is a forward-deployed AI services company: we scope one high-cost workflow, build the AI system, deploy it into your production stack, and stay accountable for it running.

The distinction matters for ROI. With Turing, a new engineer still needs to learn your stack, design the system, and build toward production - which takes months. With Altor, we have already built this system before, in stacks like yours, and we deploy in 14 days.

You have a specific high-cost workflow - support investigation, ticket triage, escalation handling - you want automated in production in the shortest possible time. You do not want to design the system; you want it running.

FAQ

Is Altor an alternative to Turing?

They solve different problems. Turing provides vetted remote engineering talent that integrates with your team. Altor is a forward-deployed AI services company that builds and operates a specific production AI system in your stack. Turing gives you a person; Altor gives you a working system.

What does Turing do that Altor does not?

Turing provides engineering talent for any kind of development work across your roadmap. Altor is focused on building and operating production AI systems for specific workflows. If you need general engineering augmentation, Turing fits. If you need one high-cost workflow automated in production in 14 days, Altor fits.

How does time to value compare between Altor and Turing?

A Turing engineer typically onboards in 1-2 weeks and then takes weeks to months to design, build, and deploy a production AI system depending on scope. Altor deploys a working production AI system in 14 days from kickoff because we bring the AI system expertise - your team does not need to design it.

When would a company use both Altor and Turing?

Some teams use Altor for a specific AI workflow automation and Turing for general engineering capacity on their product roadmap. They are complementary, not substitutes.
